Harry James Potter

The boy who lived

Harry Potter, aged 16, at the grave of Tom Riddle, after he was teleported there by Lord Voldemort through a portal hidden in the Goblet of Fire.

Here is a timeline of Harry Potter's life.

  • 1980 - born in Godric's Hollow, England
  • 1981 - attacked by Lord Voldemort who believe that the boy was destined to destroy him.Both of Harry's parents lost their lives in this attack. Harry's mother sacrificed her own life trying to save him, which created a magical barrier that protected Harry from Voldemort.
  • 1984 - Harry started living with his non-magical aunt and uncle. This proved to be rather challenging. He was not aware of his magical powers.
  • 1991 - Harry first experiences his magical potential. He discovers he can talk to snakes in an event that terrifies his family.
  • 1991 - On the day of his 11th birthday, Harry meets Hagrid and learns of his magical history. He soon starts attending Hogwarts.
  • 1992 - Harry destroys the first Horcrux - Tom Riddle's diary
  • 1993 - Harry meets Sirius Black and Remus Lupin
  • 1995 - Lord Voldemort regains his physical form, and the Black Eaters reassmble. His numbers grow.
  • 1997 - Dumbledore is killed by Snape.
  • 1997 - Harry, Ron and Hermione start looking for Horcruxes and destroying them. This goes on for two years.
  • 1998 - The Battle of Hogwarts. Voldemort is defeated and many lives lost on both sides.
  • 1999 - After finishing school Harry starts training as an auror.
  • 2005 - Harry marries Ginny Weasley.
  • 2020 - Harry becomes head of the Department of Magical Law Enforcement

"I see Harry as someone who is struggling to do the right thing, who is not without faults, who acts impetuously as you would expect someone of his age to act, but who is ultimately a very loyal person, and a very very courageous person. So, in as much as he has qualities that I admire most I would say he is a good role model. That doesn't mean that he is saintly, but then frankly, who is? But I think you do see enough of Harry's inner life, the workings of his mind in the books to know that he is ultimately human, struggling to do the right thing, which I think is admirable."

-J.K. Rowling
